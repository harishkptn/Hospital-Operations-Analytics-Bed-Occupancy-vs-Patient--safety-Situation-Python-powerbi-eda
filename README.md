# 📊 Customer Segmentation & RFM Analysis

## 🚀 Project Overview
This project focuses on analyzing e-commerce customer data to understand purchasing behavior and segment customers using **RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) analysis**.

The goal is to help businesses identify high-value customers, improve marketing strategies, and drive revenue growth through data-driven decision-making.

---

## 🧩 Problem Statement
In e-commerce businesses, not all customers contribute equally to revenue. Without proper segmentation, companies:

- Spend marketing budgets inefficiently  
- Fail to retain valuable customers  
- Miss opportunities for personalization  

👉 This project solves that by identifying **who the most valuable customers are** and how to target them effectively.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

- Analyze customer purchasing behavior  
- Perform data cleaning and exploratory data analysis (EDA)  
- Apply RFM analysis to segment customers  
- Identify high-value and low-value customer groups  
- Generate actionable business insights  

---

## 📁 Dataset Description

The dataset contains transactional data from an e-commerce platform, including:

- InvoiceNo – Transaction ID  
- StockCode – Product ID  
- Description – Product name  
- Quantity – Number of items purchased  
- InvoiceDate – Date of transaction  
- UnitPrice – Price per item  
- CustomerID – Unique customer identifier  
- Country – Customer location  

---

## ⚙️ Methodology

### 🔹 What is RFM Analysis?

- Recency (R): How recently a customer made a purchase  
- Frequency (F): How often they purchase  
- Monetary (M): How much money they spend  

---

## 🔄 Steps Involved

### 1. Data Cleaning & Preparation
- Removed missing values  
- Handled duplicate records  
- Filtered out cancelled orders  
- Converted data types  

### 2. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
- Sales trends over time  
- Product and country analysis  

### 3. Time Series Analysis
- Daily sales trends  
- Patterns across time  

### 4. Pareto Analysis (80/20 Rule)
- Identified top contributing customers and products  

### 5. Feature Engineering
- Created Total Revenue column  
- Aggregated customer-level data  

### 6. RFM Calculation
- Recency  
- Frequency  
- Monetary  

### 7. RFM Scoring
- Assigned scores (1–5)  

### 8. Customer Segmentation
- High-value customers  
- Loyal customers  
- At-risk customers  
- Lost customers  

---

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies

- Python (Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, Seaborn)  
- Jupyter Notebook  

---

## 📈 Key Insights & Findings

- 20% of customers contribute ~80% of revenue  
- High-frequency ≠ always high spending  
- Identified churn-risk customers  
- Top products drive most sales  

---

## 💡 Business Recommendations

- Target high-value customers with loyalty programs  
- Re-engage at-risk customers  
- Focus on top-performing products  
- Expand in high-performing regions  

---

## 🧠 Conclusion

This project demonstrates how transactional data can be transformed into actionable business insights using RFM analysis.
